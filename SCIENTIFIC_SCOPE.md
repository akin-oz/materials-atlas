# Scientific Scope

> Boundaries for what Materials Atlas is responsible for explaining.

## Included

Materials Atlas covers Computational Materials Science and the practical knowledge needed to enter it from a software-engineering background. Its scope includes:

- materials foundations that unlock computational reasoning
- thermodynamics, statistical mechanics, crystallography, electronic structure, and multiscale methods
- scientific computing, reproducibility, research infrastructure, and scientific software
- materials informatics, machine learning for materials, and AI for materials
- curated relationships among methods, software, datasets, researchers, laboratories, and open problems

## Excluded

Materials Atlas does not attempt to be:

- a complete materials-science textbook
- an exhaustive bibliography, software directory, or institution ranking
- a replacement for primary literature, laboratory training, or supervised research
- a source of proprietary process parameters, safety procedures, or regulatory advice
- a general-purpose chemistry, physics, machine-learning, or software-engineering curriculum

## Future Scope

New material classes, applications, and methods may enter the Atlas only when they strengthen its central purpose: helping readers navigate Computational Materials Science with defensible conceptual and research context.

Future expansion should proceed through the domain lifecycle in [domains/README.md](domains/README.md). A topic's popularity, commercial interest, or topical novelty is not sufficient reason to include it.
