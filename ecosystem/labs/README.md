# Laboratories and Institutions

This index maps institutions that shape Computational Materials Science, Materials Informatics, AI for Materials, or the infrastructure around them.

It is not a ranking and not a directory. Institutions are included when they help explain where important methods, datasets, software, research communities, or industrial directions come from.

## Selection Philosophy

An institution belongs here when its role helps readers understand the field's structure.

The focus is on durable influence: research programs, software ecosystems, datasets, facilities, educational output, and sustained contributions to scientific practice.

Use lightweight relationships:

- Associated domains: conceptual areas shaped by the institution.
- Associated infrastructure: software, datasets, workflows, or facilities connected to the institution.
- Leads to: the next concept a reader should inspect.

## Seed Institutions

### Materials Project, Berkeley

Why it belongs: The Materials Project is one of the central infrastructures for computed materials data and high-throughput materials discovery.

How to read its role: Treat it as both a data platform and a research program that shaped how materials computation became searchable, reusable, and programmable.

Associated domains: Materials Informatics, Density Functional Theory, Research Infrastructure.

Associated infrastructure: Materials Project, pymatgen, atomate lineage, high-throughput DFT.

Leads to: reusable computed materials data and database-driven discovery.

### MIT

Why it belongs: MIT has broad influence across computational materials, mechanics, molecular simulation, materials design, and AI-connected materials research.

How to read its role: Use MIT as an institutional node for connecting materials science, computation, mechanics, and modern machine learning directions.

Associated domains: Computational Materials, Molecular Dynamics, AI for Materials.

Associated infrastructure: multiscale modeling, mechanics-informed materials design.

Leads to: structure-property modeling and AI-assisted materials research.

### Northwestern University

Why it belongs: Northwestern has long-standing strength in computational materials, alloy design, phase stability, high-throughput methods, and materials discovery.

How to read its role: Study Northwestern-linked work when learning how first-principles computation, thermodynamics, and materials design connect.

Associated domains: Density Functional Theory, CALPHAD, Materials Informatics.

Associated infrastructure: high-throughput computation, alloy design, phase stability research.

Leads to: computational thermodynamics and materials discovery.

### Carnegie Mellon University

Why it belongs: Carnegie Mellon is important for materials science, data-driven materials research, process-structure-property thinking, and computational approaches connected to engineering practice.

How to read its role: Use CMU as a node for understanding materials data, processing, microstructure, and engineering integration.

Associated domains: Materials Informatics, Phase-Field, Computational Materials.

Associated infrastructure: process-structure-property modeling and data-driven materials research.

Leads to: microstructure-aware informatics and engineering integration.

### NIST

Why it belongs: NIST contributes standards, measurements, materials data, workshops, and field-level coordination around AI for materials and materials information infrastructure.

How to read its role: Study NIST when learning how materials data, standards, uncertainty, and trust connect to computational and AI-driven research.

Associated domains: Research Infrastructure, Materials Informatics, AI for Materials.

Associated infrastructure: standards, FAIR principles, uncertainty, AIMS workshops.

Leads to: trustworthy materials data and reproducible AI-for-materials practice.

### Argonne National Laboratory

Why it belongs: Argonne combines computational science, materials research, high-performance computing, and large-scale experimental infrastructure.

How to read its role: Use Argonne to understand how computational materials connects to national-lab facilities, characterization, simulation, and applied research programs.

Associated domains: Computational Materials, Research Infrastructure.

Associated infrastructure: HPC, large-scale facilities, computation-characterization workflows.

Leads to: national-lab-scale computational and experimental integration.

### Microsoft AI for Science

Why it belongs: Microsoft AI for Science is a major industrial research effort applying AI to scientific modeling, including materials-relevant problems.

How to read its role: Study it as an example of how large-scale industrial AI research frames scientific discovery, simulation, and foundation-model-style approaches.

Associated domains: AI for Materials, Research Infrastructure.

Associated infrastructure: AI for Science, MatterGen, large-scale scientific modeling.

Leads to: generative materials design and AI-driven discovery systems.

### Google DeepMind

Why it belongs: Google DeepMind has produced high-profile AI-for-materials work, including large-scale materials discovery and generative materials models.

How to read its role: Study it as a major industrial AI actor whose claims should be evaluated through datasets, validation, synthesis feasibility, and scientific adoption.

Associated domains: AI for Materials, Materials Informatics.

Associated infrastructure: GNoME, large-scale materials discovery, model-driven screening.

Leads to: foundation-model-style materials discovery and validation questions.

## Inclusion Criteria

Future additions should satisfy at least one of these conditions:

- the institution maintains important scientific infrastructure
- the institution leads a durable research program
- the institution produces widely used software or datasets
- the institution hosts facilities central to computational or data-driven materials research
- the institution shapes standards, reproducibility, or community practice

If an institution cannot be connected to specific domains, tools, datasets, or research programs, do not add it.
