# Materials Atlas v0.1.0

Materials Atlas v0.1.0 is the first public release of an open knowledge system for Computational Materials Science.

## Vision

The repository is designed to help software engineers with a materials background build research-ready understanding of Computational Materials Science. It favors conceptual navigation, carefully chosen resources, reusable references, and explicit learning progression over exhaustive collection.

## Scope

This release establishes the project's editorial and structural foundation:

- a high-level field roadmap
- a detailed 16-module transition curriculum
- reusable thermodynamics and statistical-mechanics references
- a canonical Mermaid diagram library
- curated resource and ecosystem indexes
- research-practice and scientific-software foundations
- licensing and citation guidance for reuse and academic reference

## Highlights

- The curriculum moves from mathematical recovery and materials foundations through thermodynamics, simulation, informatics, research infrastructure, and a capstone project.
- Canonical references reduce repeated explanations and give future domain pages stable material to link to.
- The diagram library assigns stable identifiers and conceptual ownership to reusable visual models.
- Editorial standards define what belongs in the Atlas and what should be excluded.

## Limitations

Materials Atlas is intentionally incomplete.

- It does not yet include mature domain pages.
- It does not replace textbooks, primary literature, or supervised research training.
- It does not attempt to be an exhaustive bibliography, software directory, or institutional ranking.
- Some curriculum resources are introduced as orientation points rather than fully developed reading pathways.

These limits are part of the release scope. New content should earn inclusion by making the field easier to understand.

## Future Direction

Future work will prioritize carefully edited domain pages, stronger cross-links between canonical references and learning modules, and improvements driven by real learner use. A Zenodo archive and DOI will be added after the release is preserved.
